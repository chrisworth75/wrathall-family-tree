The Wrathall Family

Compiled from the memoir of Leslie Gordon Wrathall, 1967

One day I got bored at work and wondered if there was any truth to a tale that had been handed down through the generations of my family. The story was that my great grandma Constance's father was born from an affair with one of the Lowthers of Lowther Castle, which is why the name Lonsdale appears as one of Constance's middle names. It's from the hereditary title the Earl of Lonsdale holds. All I needed to do was type 'Lonsdale Wrathall' into Google and the first result was this excellent memoir written by Constance's younger brother, Leslie Gordon Wrathall: wrathall.org

I was astonished to read the memoir, because my grandparents are mentioned in it but also because it seemed familiar. Back in 2000 when my grandad Arthur Gordon Worthington died we were given a suitcase full of his photographs and letters, and the memoir must have been in that. Annoyingly we cannot find the suitcase now, which is why I was delighted to find that Hamish had managed to get it published on wrathall.org, for future generations (me!) to find.

This all happened during Covid times, and it took me until this year (2025) to get round to sending Hamish an email. He responded! I also reached out to Kathy, the daughter of Evelyn Constance Irene Wrathall (Auntie Renie) who my Auntie Ros was still in touch with. Then my sister told me she had received a message on ancestry.co.uk from a Daniel Shaw in Canada who was a descendant of Henry Lonsdale Wrathall. I was pretty excited about this so I subscribed and made contact. Daniel seems to be just as interested in the story as I am.

At the time of writing it's less than 12 hours after making contact so I am quickly publishing this tree that I put together from Leslie's memoir. I'm hoping Daniel and I can collaborate on extending the tree downwards and maybe upwards, but finding any proof on the Lowther connection will be much more difficult...
